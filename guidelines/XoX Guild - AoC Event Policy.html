Area of Conquest (AoC) Official Policy ⚔️

To maximize our collective rewards and ensure fair participation, Guild XoX has established a formal policy for the AoC event. All members are expected to contribute to the growth of our Castle.

1. The Goal of AoC Cooperation

The primary objective of AoC is not just individual ranking, but the leveling up of the Guild Castle. Higher Castle levels provide more rewards for every single member. Therefore, hoarding coins or zero-contribution behavior hinders the progress of the entire guild.

2. Contribution Standards

We have established two tiers of contribution to ensure both participation and growth:

Category Minimum Coin Threshold Description & Expectation
Minimum Participation 5,000 Coins Easily achievable through quest rewards and collecting coins via combat and settlement capture.
Active Contributor 15,000+ Coins Our target for active growth. Members reaching this help the guild unlock higher-tier rewards.

3. Mandatory Daily Actions

  • Daily Combat: Engage in at least one fight per day (win or lose).
  • Settlement Management: Try to capture or hold at least one settlement to generate coins.
  • The Castle Rule: All collected coins MUST be invested into the Castle upgrades before the event ends.

4. Warnings & Expulsion Policy

We monitor individual contributions through the event log. The following rules apply:

  • Zero Contribution (0 Coins): Immediate "Yellow Card" warning. If no valid reason is provided (e.g., connection issues, personal emergency), a second occurrence will lead to immediate expulsion.
  • Repeated Low Contribution: Members who consistently stay below the 5,000 threshold without prior notice will be reviewed for potential removal.

5. Exceptions & Communication

  • We understand that real-life issues (internet outages, national circumstances, etc.) can occur.
  • If you are unable to participate, please inform the Guild Leader or Generals via PM or Guild Chat as soon as possible.
  • Exemptions will be granted for documented personal/local issues.
